Evaluating Offline RL Algorithms on Minari with NdLinear Layers

Ashwin K M
Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore
TL;DR: We analyze the performance of various reinforcement learning algorithms across different simulation environments, demonstrating that our proposed modifications consistently outperform baseline implementations.

Motivation

We present a comprehensive benchmark study comparing the performance of popular offline reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms across standard Gymnasium MuJoCo environments: HalfCheetah, Hopper, and Walker2d. The methods evaluated include:

  1. Decision Transformer (DT)
  2. Implicit Q Learning(IQL)
  3. Behavior Cloning (BC)
  4. TD3-BC
  5. AWAC
  6. ReBRAC
Our primary objective is to investigate the impact of replacing traditional fully connected layers (nn.Linear) with NdLinear layers, which preserve the multi-dimensional structure of input data.

NdLinear introduces several advantages:

  1. Improved representational capacity
  2. Parameter efficiency
  3. Structure-aware transformations
  4. Minimal computational overhead
By integrating NdLinear into existing RL models, we evaluate its effectiveness in enhancing policy performance without increasing architectural complexity. Our results show that NdLinear can outperform nn.Linear in several scenarios, establishing it as a promising drop-in replacement for deep RL pipelines.

Algorithms

Explore our collection of algorithms with interactive visualizations and detailed analysis.

Behaviour Cloning(1.0)

When trained on the full dataset, Behavior Cloning benefits from comprehensive coverage of expert behavior, enabling more accurate action prediction and stronger generalization within the demonstrated states.

Although still susceptible to compounding errors over extended horizons, full-data BC performs reliably in well-covered, dense-reward settings and provides a robust baseline for offline reinforcement learning.

Its simplicity and effectiveness make it a common starting point for evaluating more advanced offline RL methods.

Behaviour Cloning(0.1)

Training Behavior Cloning with only 10% of the available demonstration data often results in limited policy effectiveness.

The reduced data coverage restricts the model’s ability to generalize, leading to poor performance when encountering states outside the training distribution.

This limitation causes errors to accumulate quickly over long sequences, reducing reliability in complex tasks.

TD3 - BC

TD3+BC enhances the TD3 (Twin Delayed DDPG) framework for offline learning by introducing a behavior cloning regularization term into the policy objective.

This combination allows the policy to maximize Q-values while staying close to the actions observed in the dataset, preventing divergence.

The method achieves strong performance on continuous control tasks using suboptimal data, and serves as a strong foundation for more advanced methods.

ReBRAC

ReBRAC (Revisited Behavior Regularized Actor Critic) builds upon TD3+BC with a series of design improvements aimed at better stability and efficiency.

These include deeper neural networks, layer normalization in critic networks, a high discount factor, and decoupled policy regularization.

ReBRAC achieves state-of-the-art results on numerous D4RL and robotic benchmarks, while maintaining architectural simplicity and fast convergence.

AWAC

AWAC (Advantage Weighted Actor Critic) introduces a principled way to prioritize better-than-average actions by weighting them exponentially according to their estimated advantages.

This strategy allows the policy to improve while remaining close to the behavior policy, avoiding distribution shift without requiring its explicit estimation.

AWAC has demonstrated impressive performance in sparse-reward tasks and real-world robotic control with limited interaction.

Implicit Q Learning(IQL)

Implicit Q-Learning (IQL) tackles the offline RL challenge of out-of-distribution actions by avoiding the standard Q-learning max operator.

Instead, it uses expectile regression to learn a conservative value function and guides policy improvement implicitly through a weighted regression framework.

This design allows IQL to perform effectively with suboptimal datasets and achieve strong results without explicit policy constraints.

Decision Transformer(DT)

Decision Transformers reframe reinforcement learning as a sequence modeling problem, applying transformer architectures to predict actions based on past (state, action, return-to-go) sequences.

By leveraging the power of generative modeling and causal attention, they produce policies that can generalize across long horizons and varying return conditions.

This approach has shown robustness in sparse-reward environments and compatibility with multimodal inputs like images.
